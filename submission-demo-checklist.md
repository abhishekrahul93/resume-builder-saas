# ClauseRisk India Submission Demo Checklist

## 30-Second Mobile Test

Open https://clauserisk-india.vercel.app on your phone.

1. Tap **Use Sample Contract**.
2. Confirm the risk report loads without needing another submit button.
3. Check that High Risk cards are easy to scan.
4. Select **Legal Notice** tone and run the sample again.
5. Confirm the generated script sounds formal and usable.
6. Tap **Share with CA / lawyer on WhatsApp** after the report appears.

## 60-Second Demo Video Script

1. Start on the homepage.
   Say: "ClauseRisk India is a Gemini-powered legal agent for Indian SMEs."

2. Show the **Reviewing as** toggle.
   Say: "Risk is perspective-dependent, so a founder can review as Vendor or Client."

3. Show **Negotiation tone**.
   Say: "The agent can generate a Polite, Firm, or Legal Notice style negotiation script."

4. Click **Use Sample Contract**.
   Say: "Busy reviewers can see the product work in one click."

5. Point to MSME/payment risk.
   Say: "The agent catches 60/90/120-day payment clauses and flags MSMED Act payment exposure, including potential compound interest risk where applicable."

6. Show generated script.
   Say: "The product does not just identify risk. It helps the founder act on it."

7. Show WhatsApp share.
   Say: "The report can be shared with a CA or lawyer, which matches how Indian SMEs actually make legal decisions."

## Application Numbers To Collect

Track these before submission:

- Testers contacted
- Testers who used the demo
- Contracts reviewed
- Average high-risk clauses per contract
- Most useful risk flag
- Willingness to pay: Rs. 499 / Rs. 999 / Rs. 1,999
- Anonymous testimonial quotes

## Social Proof Rule

Do not invent social proof. Add one anonymized quote to the site only after a real tester gives permission.

Good format:

"Flagged a 90-day payment clause I had missed." - SaaS founder, Bengaluru

## Strong Traction Sentence Template

In beta, ClauseRisk India analyzed [X] contracts for [Y] Indian SME/founder testers, identifying an average of [Z] high-risk clauses per document. The most cited risk was [payment exposure / indemnity / dispute venue], and [N] testers said they would pay Rs. [499/999/1999] per review.
